Martinique — Caribbean island of culture, creole cuisine, and verdant peaks

Martinique is an island of volcanic mountains, rainforests, and historic French-Caribbean culture. As the place of my father's family origin, it holds personal significance; I have not yet been, and this site collects research and itineraries toward a first trip. The text below gives practical highlights and curated activities.

Book Now Request Info

Language

French and Martinican Creole; many cultural institutions are bilingual.

Climate

Tropical; warm year-round with a wet season (June–November).

Top Sights

Mount Pelée, Les Salines beach, Saint-Pierre historic district.

Quick facts

Area

About 1,128 km²—lively islands and much coastline to explore.

Cuisine

Creole flavors, seafood specialities, French-influenced pastries.

Access

International flights via Fort-de-France; local ferry and road networks.
